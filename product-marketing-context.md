## POSITIONING

**Unique Mechanism:**
The Sentinel Certification System™

Three phases that no off-the-shelf platform replicates:

Phase 1 — Precision Gap Assessment
Every team member completes a structured diagnostic before training begins.
We map individual knowledge gaps to exact CompTIA exam domain objectives.
No one sits through content they already know. No one skips content they're missing.
This is why our first-attempt pass rate is 96% — not because we're optimistic,
but because we refuse to let anyone sit the real exam before they're ready.

Phase 2 — Instructor-Led Certification Training
Live sessions led by CISSP, CASP+, and CySA+ credentialed instructors.
Curriculum mapped 1:1 to CompTIA exam domains — not general cybersecurity theory.
Three sessions per week, two hours each. Real instructors, real questions, real accountability.

Phase 3 — Exam Simulation and Readiness Sign-Off
600+ practice questions in CompTIA exam format.
Timed mock exams under real conditions.
Weekly performance reports delivered to the CISO or training lead.
Individual readiness sign-off issued before exam scheduling.
No team member sits the live exam until scoring consistently above 85%.

**What Makes the Mechanism Different:**
Every other training vendor sells access to content.
Sentinel sells a certified team — and backs it with a contractual guarantee.
The mechanism removes the single biggest risk: paying for training and still failing.
The CIO can put our pass guarantee in the audit file. That's the sale.

## COMPETITION

**Primary Competitors:**

SANS Institute
Strengths: Gold standard reputation in enterprise security; highest industry credibility.
Weaknesses: Extremely expensive ($3,000–$7,000 per individual); not team-focused;
not designed around compliance audit documentation.
How Sentinel wins: Team contracts at fraction of per-seat SANS cost;
compliance-first approach; faster turnaround (7 weeks vs months).

Pluralsight / LinkedIn Learning / Udemy for Business
Strengths: Low cost; large content library; familiar to L&D buyers.
Weaknesses: Self-paced means teams don't finish; no exam accountability; no pass guarantee;
completion certificates mean nothing to CompTIA auditors.
How Sentinel wins: We sell certified teams, not access to content.
Completion rates on platforms average 12%. Our exam pass rate is 96%.

Generic IT Training Vendors (New Horizons, Global Knowledge)
Strengths: Long-established; recognized names in corporate IT training.
Weaknesses: Generalist curriculum not specialized in security certifications;
no compliance-aware positioning; individual enrollment model.
How Sentinel wins: Security-only focus; team contract model; compliance documentation
as a deliverable; faster path to certification.

Internal Training Programs
Strengths: Zero incremental cost (perceived); uses existing staff.
Weaknesses: No structured exam-prep methodology; no accountability; no time commitment;
existing staff have day jobs; no certification guarantee.
How Sentinel wins: Compliance auditors don't accept "we trained internally."
They ask for cert numbers and exam dates. Internal programs don't produce those.
