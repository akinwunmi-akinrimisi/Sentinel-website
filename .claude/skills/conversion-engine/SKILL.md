# Conversion Engine Skill

High-ticket conversion is fundamentally different from mass-market conversion.
Price is not the objection. Trust, specificity, and certainty are.

The buyer at £10K–£100K is not looking for the best deal.
They are looking for the only person/service that can solve their specific problem.
Your job: make them feel found.

---

## The High-Ticket Conversion Formula

```
Specific Pain → Unique Mechanism → Specific Result → Credibility → Low-Risk Next Step
```

Every page section must serve one of these five stages.
Every section that serves none of them must be removed.

---

## Copy Principles

### 1. Specificity Beats Claims
Every vague claim must be replaced with a specific fact.

| ❌ Vague | ✅ Specific |
|---|---|
| "Proven results" | "47 clients. £180M raised. Average 14-week close." |
| "Expert in fundraising" | "Former Goldman Sachs VP. Closed 32 deals over 11 years." |
| "Fast results" | "Series A ready in 90 days — or we work for free until you are." |
| "Trusted by leaders" | "Trusted by founders at Monzo, Zilch, and GoCardless." |
| "Premium service" | "Maximum 4 clients per quarter. Currently 1 spot available." |

### 2. The Buyer Is Not Buying the Service — They Are Buying the Outcome
Never lead with what you do. Lead with what they get.

```
❌ "We provide strategic advisory services for growth-stage founders."
✅ "Your next raise. Your terms. No equity given up unnecessarily."
```

### 3. Address the Real Objection — Not the Stated One
High-ticket buyers rarely say the price is too high.
They say "I need to think about it." What they mean is:
- "I'm not sure this will work for my specific situation."
- "I don't know if I can trust you."
- "I've been burned before."

Write copy that addresses these, not the price.

### 4. Scarcity Must Be Real
Never manufacture fake scarcity (countdown timers, "24 spots left" that reset).
Real scarcity that converts at high-ticket:
- Capacity limit: "We take 4 clients per quarter. We're currently filling Q3."
- Time constraint: "Our onboarding closes on [date] for the next cohort."
- Access: "By application only — not everyone is accepted."

### 5. The Guarantee Removes the Last Objection
Every high-ticket offer must have a guarantee or risk reversal.
The guarantee does not need to be "full refund."
Examples:
- "If we don't identify £500K in revenue opportunities in your first 30 days, we refund your entire investment."
- "We work with you until you've closed your raise. No time limit."
- "If after 30 days you don't feel this is the highest-leverage investment you've made, we'll refund in full."

## Section-by-Section Copy Framework

### Hero Section

**Structure:**
```
[Eyebrow label — context setter]
[Primary headline — 5–8 words, outcome-focused]
[Sub-headline — 1–2 sentences, agitate + promise]
[CTA — action verb + specific outcome]
[Trust proof — 1 line below CTA]
```

**Headline formulas:**
1. Outcome without sacrifice: `"Raise £5M Without Giving Up Your Board Seat"`
2. Provocative statement: `"Your Pitch Deck Isn't the Problem."`
3. For the specific person: `"The Advisory Firm 7-Figure Founders Actually Use"`
4. Result + timeframe: `"Series A Ready in 90 Days"`
5. Bold claim: `"We've Helped Founders Raise £180M. Here's What We Know."`

**CTA formulas (avoid "Submit" or "Learn More"):**
- `"Book Your Strategy Call"` — specific action
- `"Apply for a Spot"` — scarcity + selectivity
- `"Get the Framework"` — value exchange
- `"See If You Qualify"` — reframes it as their opportunity

### Problem / Agitation Section

**Purpose:** Make the reader feel understood before you offer the solution.
**Rule:** Use their language, not yours.

**Framework:**
```
You're not struggling because you lack ambition.
You're struggling because [specific systemic problem].

[Pain 1]: [Specific scenario they've experienced]
[Pain 2]: [Specific scenario they've experienced]
[Pain 3]: [Specific scenario they've experienced]

The worst part? [Twist — why the common solution makes it worse]
```

### Solution / Offer Section

**Structure:**
```
[Name of the offer — give it a distinctive name]
[One-sentence description — what it is + who it's for + the outcome]
[The unique mechanism — named, explained briefly]
[What's included — specific, not vague]
[What happens after — the clear next step]
```

**Presenting what's included — luxury format:**
```
Not a list of features.
A description of the transformation each element produces.

❌ "Weekly 1:1 coaching calls"
✅ "Weekly strategy sessions where we reverse-engineer your raise — from investor
    psychology to term sheet structure — so you walk into every meeting knowing
    exactly what they need to hear."
```

### Pricing Section

**Structure:**
```
[Reframe: investment, not cost]
[The price — stated clearly, not buried]
[ROI anchor — what typical clients see vs. the investment]
[What's included — brief, not exhaustive]
[Payment options — if applicable]
[Guarantee]
[CTA]
```

**The ROI anchor format:**
```
Your investment: £24,000
Typical outcome: £3M–£8M raised in 14 weeks
Most clients recoup their investment in the first week after close.
```

**Pricing language:**
```
❌ "Cost" — implies expense
✅ "Investment" — implies return

❌ "Price" — commodity language
✅ "Your investment" — ownership language

❌ "Cheap / affordable option" — do not use
✅ "Payment schedule available" — for options
```

### Testimonials — Copy Structure

Each testimonial should follow this structure in the editorial around it:

```
[Client name, title, company — full, never initials]
[The situation before]
[The specific result with numbers]
[The quote — 2–4 sentences in their voice]
```

**Write the intro copy around testimonials:**
```
❌ "Here's what our clients say:"
✅ "In the last 18 months, our clients have raised £180M combined.
    These are three of their stories."
```

### FAQ — Copy Framework

**Question ordering:**
1. What is this exactly? (Clarity)
2. Who is this for? (Qualification)
3. Who is this NOT for? (Anti-qualification — builds trust)
4. How does it work? (Process)
5. What results can I expect? (Outcomes)
6. How long does it take? (Time investment)
7. Why is it [price]? (Price justification)
8. Is there a guarantee? (Risk reversal)
9. How do I get started? (Next step)
10. What if I'm not sure it's right for me? (Objection handler → CTA)

**Answer formula:**
- First sentence: Direct answer to the question. No preamble.
- Next 1–2 sentences: Specific context or example.
- Last sentence: Bridge to the next step or related point.

### About / Founder Copy

**Framework:**
```
[Bold credential statement — not biographical]
[Specific result you produced — with numbers]
[Why you do this — human moment, not mission statement]
[Who you exist to serve — specific]
[Social proof markers — logos, press, numbers]
```

**Example:**
```
❌ "Sarah has been passionate about helping entrepreneurs for over a decade.
    She believes every founder deserves access to world-class strategy."

✅ "Twelve years at Goldman Sachs. £180M in deals closed. Left to work with
    the founders the institutions won't touch.

    I've sat across from every tier of institutional investor. I know what they
    actually want — and it's almost never what's in your deck.

    Now I work with 4 founders per quarter. That's it. You get everything I know."
```

## CTA Button Copy

Never use default button text. Every CTA must be specific.

| Context | ❌ Generic | ✅ Specific |
|---|---|---|
| Hero | "Get Started" | "Book Your Strategy Call" |
| Pricing | "Buy Now" | "Secure Your Spot" |
| Application | "Apply" | "See If You Qualify" |
| Lead magnet | "Download" | "Get the Framework" |
| Contact | "Submit" | "Send My Message" |
| Confirmation | "Done" | "You're In — Check Your Email" |

---

## Conversion Killers — Never Do These

- ❌ Using "We" more than "You" in the first 100 words
- ❌ Starting with the company name or history
- ❌ Leading with features before outcomes
- ❌ More than one primary CTA per section
- ❌ Star ratings on testimonials (retail signal, not premium)
- ❌ Unattributed testimonials (initials only = not trustworthy)
- ❌ Countdown timers that reset (visible manipulation)
- ❌ "Click here" or "Learn more" as link/button text
- ❌ More than 3 navigation items in the header (reduces focus)
- ❌ Anything that looks like a pop-up on first load
- ❌ Social share buttons (distracts from the single conversion goal)
- ❌ Prices with too many options (paradox of choice kills high-ticket)

## Copy Review Checklist

Before finalizing any page or section:

- [ ] Hero headline is 5–8 words and outcome-focused
- [ ] Sub-headline adds specificity — not a restatement of the headline
- [ ] Primary CTA has specific action language
- [ ] At least one piece of proof above the fold (numbers, logos, quote)
- [ ] "You" appears more than "We" in the first 200 words
- [ ] Pricing section states ROI anchor alongside price
- [ ] Guarantee is explicitly stated
- [ ] Scarcity signal is real and specific
- [ ] FAQ addresses price objection directly
- [ ] No vague words: "proven," "expert," "world-class," "passionate" — replace with specifics
- [ ] Language matches WEBSITE_CONTEXT.md tone direction
- [ ] Phrases from product-marketing-context.md "phrases to avoid" are not present
- [ ] Every section has a clear next micro-action (even if not a CTA button)
